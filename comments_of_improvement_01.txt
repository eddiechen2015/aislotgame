Follow-up — the math-tuning round behind this 👇

  Goal: land a 96.2% RTP target without knocking the rest of the
  math profile off-spec.

  Starting profile: total RTP ~91.4%, hit frequency ~48.6%,
  base/free-spin split off-target.

  Process:
  • Automated RTP search over candidate paytable/reel profiles
    (common random numbers + adaptive racing)
  • Tuner pass to lock the base vs free-spin RTP contribution
  • Batch verification: 5 seeds × 4M = 20M spins (MGA market),
    with mean / 95% CI / normalized-error reported per metric

  Result (Monte-Carlo verified on the engine, not live production):
  • Total RTP:        96.45%  (target 96.2%, inside tolerance)
  • Base / FS split:  57.7% / 38.7%  (targets 58% / 38.2%)
  • Hit frequency:    30.9%  (down from 48.6%)
  • Free-spin freq:   ~1 in 117 spins
  • Volatility (σ ×bet): ~22  (medium-high)
  • Max observed win: ~11,077×  (cap 30,000×)

  The point wasn't "make the number read 96.2%." Matching total RTP
  is easy — matching total RTP *and* the base/FS split *and* hit
  frequency *and* volatility *and* exposure caps at once is the real
  engineering problem. Every candidate has to clear confidence-interval
  checks before it can be promoted to an approved profile.

  Caveat: these are simulation figures; the live runtime still needs
  the approved profile wired in (+ lab-grade RNG) before any
  real-money RTP claim.
